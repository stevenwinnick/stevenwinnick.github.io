About Me

I'm a innovator and engineer excited about building the next generation of tech products. Last summer, I worked as a Technology Strategy Intern at Paramount, helping define the Paramount+/PlutoTV innovation strategy to compete in the escalating "Streaming Wars." The summer before, I was a Software Engineer and Product Manager intern at Sonar Health, where I designed and built core features of a platform that gives users information about their health with industry-leading precision. I'm originally from a smaller town outside St. Paul, MN, and throughout middle and high school, I took my math classes at the University of Minnesota as part of the U's Talented Youth Mathematics Program

Right now, I'm a Junior at Columbia University, where I'm a John Jay Scholar in the Undergraduate Scholars Program. I'm majoring in Computer Science with an Intelligent Systems specialization, which allows me to pursue my interests in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning with graduate-level coursework exploring both the theoretical and applied sides of these fields. I'm especially interested in the application of Machine Learning to the field of Robotics, and I'm an active participant in Columbia's Robotics Studio, where I'm building a walking robot capable of object detection.

When not working on my more academic interests, I can often be found making music with my MIDI keyboard or exploring New York City with my friends. I also love to cook elaborate meals and ski when I'm home in the winter.
